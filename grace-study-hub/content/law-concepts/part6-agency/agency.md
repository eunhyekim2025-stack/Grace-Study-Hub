# Agency

An agent forms legal relations with a third party on behalf of the principal. A major exception to [[wiki/law-concepts/privity]].

## 1. Creation of Agency

| Method | Description | Case |
|------|------|------|
| **Express / Implied agreement** | Express or implied agreement | — |
| **Actual authority** | Authority actually conferred by the principal (express/implied) | *Hely-Hutchinson v Brayhead [1968]* |
| **Apparent / Ostensible authority** | Third party relies on the principal's representation (estoppel) | *Freeman & Lockyer v Buckhurst [1964]* |
| **Ratification** | The principal subsequently approves an unauthorised act | *Bolton Partners v Lambert [1889]* |
| **Necessity** | Agency in an emergency situation | *Great Northern Railway v Swaffield [1874]* |
| **Estoppel** | Creating an appearance of authority | — |

## 2. Effect

- **Disclosed principal**: contract between principal and third party; the agent is, as a rule, not liable
- **Undisclosed principal**: the principal may intervene, and the third party may also elect between the principal and the agent

## 3. Duties of Agent
- Comply with instructions, exercise reasonable care and skill, **fiduciary duties**: no conflict of interest, maintain confidentiality, do not make a secret profit, act in the principal's interest

## 4. Rights of Agent
- Remuneration, indemnity for expenses, lien

## 5. Termination
- By act of the parties: agreement, revocation, renunciation
- By operation of law: death, mental incapacity, or bankruptcy of the principal/agent; frustration; expiry of the term
