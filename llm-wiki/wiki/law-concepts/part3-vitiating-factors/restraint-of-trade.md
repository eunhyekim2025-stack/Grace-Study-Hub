# Restraint of Trade

A clause restricting freedom of trade or competition is **prima facie void**, but valid if reasonable → *Nordenfelt v Maxim Nordenfelt [1894]*

## Contexts of application
- Employment contracts: post-employment non-competition (scrutinised most strictly)
- Sale of a business: the seller's non-competition covenant
- Solus agreement: an exclusive supply arrangement → *Esso v Harper's Garage [1968]*

## Two-stage validity test

### 1. Legitimate Proprietary Interest
Mere exclusion of competition is not allowed. Protectable interests:
- Trade secrets / confidential information, customer connection, workforce stability
- → *Man Financial v Wong Bark Chuan [2008] SGCA* (the leading Singapore case)

### 2. Reasonableness
- **Reasonableness between the parties**: scope, duration and area within what is necessary to protect the interest → *Mason v Provident Clothing [1913]*
- **Reasonableness in the public interest**: must not be contrary to the public interest

---

## Blue Pencil Test
If the excessive part can be severed by **deletion alone** and the remainder stands on its own with meaning, the remaining clause is valid. Rewriting by adding or amending words is not allowed. → *Goldsoll v Goldman [1915]*
