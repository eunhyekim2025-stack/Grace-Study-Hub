# Remedies

Remedies for breach of contract. **Common law remedies (as of right)** + **equitable remedies (discretionary)**.

## Damages

### Liquidated vs Unliquidated
- **Liquidated damages**: a sum of loss fixed in advance in the contract. Valid if a genuine pre-estimate of loss; void if a **penalty** → *Dunlop v New Garage [1915]*; Singapore: *Denka Advantech v Seraya Energy [2020] SGCA* (retains the penalty doctrine)
- **Unliquidated damages**: assessed by the court

### The 4 principles of damages

1. **Causation**: the breach caused the loss
2. **Remoteness**: two limbs (*Hadley v Baxendale [1854]*) — (a) ordinary losses, (b) losses where special circumstances were known
3. **Measure**: expectation interest — to put the party in the position as if performed. Alternative: reliance interest → *Robinson v Harman [1848]*
4. **Mitigation**: must reduce the loss by reasonable steps → *British Westinghouse v Underground [1912]*

- **Non-pecuniary loss**: generally unavailable, exception (contracts for peace of mind / enjoyment) → *Jarvis v Swans Tours [1973]*

## Equitable Remedies (discretionary, where damages are inadequate)

| Remedy | Content | Authority |
|------|------|------|
| **Specific performance** | Compelling performance (specific goods / land) | *Beswick v Beswick [1968]* |
| **Injunction** | An order to do / refrain from doing | *Warner Bros v Nelson [1937]* |
| **Rescission** | Setting aside the contract / restitution | (where a vitiating factor exists) |
| **Rectification** | Correction of the writing | — |

## Other
- **Restitution**: recovery of unjust enrichment (*quantum meruit / quantum valebat*)
- **Action for an agreed sum**: claim for the agreed price
