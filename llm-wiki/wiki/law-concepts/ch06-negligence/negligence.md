# Negligence

A type of tort. The 3 elements: **duty of care · breach of duty · damage (including causation)**.

## 1. Duty of Care

Origin: the neighbour principle → *Donoghue v Stevenson [1932]*

### Singapore — Spandeck 2-stage test (*Spandeck Engineering v DSTA [2007] SGCA*)
Threshold requirement: **factual foreseeability**
1. **Proximity**: physical, circumstantial and causal proximity + voluntary assumption of responsibility & reliance
2. **Policy considerations**: once stage 1 is met, examine any policy reasons to deny the duty

> Singapore adopts a **single, unified test** (applied to all types of loss) instead of England's 3-stage Caparo test.

## 2. Breach of Duty

Standard: the **reasonable person** → *Blyth v Birmingham Waterworks [1856]*
- Factors: likelihood and seriousness of the harm, cost of precautions, social utility of the conduct
- Professionals: the standard of a similar professional → *Bolam v Friern [1957]* (modified for medical advice in *Hii Chii Kok [2017]*)

## 3. Damage (+ causation)

- **Factual causation**: the "but for" test → *Barnett v Chelsea Hospital [1969]*
- **Legal causation / Remoteness**: reasonable foreseeability → *The Wagon Mound (No 1) [1961]*
- **Types of loss**: personal injury / property damage (readily recognised) vs **pure economic loss** (restricted, *Spandeck*) vs nervous shock

## Defences
- **Contributory negligence**: apportionment of loss → [[wiki/statutes/contributory-negligence-act]]
- **Volenti non fit injuria**: voluntary assumption of risk
