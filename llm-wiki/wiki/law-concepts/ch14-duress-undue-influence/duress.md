# Duress

Coercing consent to a contract through illegitimate pressure. Renders the contract **voidable**.

## Types

| Type | Description | Authority |
|------|------|------|
| **Duress to the person** | A threat to body, life or liberty | *Barton v Armstrong [1976]* |
| **Duress of goods** | A threat to property or goods | *Skeate v Beale [1840]* |
| **Economic duress** | Economic pressure | *The Atlantic Baron [1979]*, *Pao On v Lau Yiu Long [1980]* |

## Economic Duress — 2 requirements (Singapore: *Tam Tak Chuen v Khairul [2009]*, *E C Investment v Ridout [2011]*)

### 1. Illegitimate pressure
- An unlawful act (breach of contract / tort) or an illegitimate threat
- Mere commercial pressure is insufficient → *Universe Tankships v ITWF [1983]*

### 2. Causation
- The pressure must be a **significant cause** of entering the contract
- Factors (*Pao On*): (a) whether there was protest, (b) availability of alternatives, (c) independent advice, (d) any subsequent attempt to set aside

---

## Effects
- The contract is voidable → the victim may rescind
- Bars to rescission: affirmation, lapse of time, third party rights
