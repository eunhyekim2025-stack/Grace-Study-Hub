# Terms of Contract

## Pre-contractual Statements

Distinguishing whether a statement is a **term** of the contract or a mere **representation**.

| Factor | Description | Authority |
|-----------|------|------|
| Timing | The time gap between the statement and the formation of the contract | *Routledge v McKay [1954]* |
| Importance | Whether the statement was central to the contract | *Bannerman v White [1861]* |
| Special knowledge / skill | Where the maker has special expertise, more likely to be a term | *Oscar Chess v Williams [1957]*, *Dick Bentley v Harold Smith [1965]* |
| Whether reduced to writing | If included in a written contract, it is a term | *Routledge v McKay* |

## Parol Evidence Rule

Where a written contract is complete, oral/extrinsic evidence that varies or adds to it is excluded.
- **Evidence Act s.93**: the writing is the only evidence
- **Evidence Act s.94**: extrinsic evidence cannot vary the terms of the writing
- See [[wiki/statutes/evidence-act]]

### Exceptions (s.94 provisos (a)-(f))
- Vitiating factors, separate oral agreements, conditional operation, custom/trade usage, resolving ambiguity, etc.

## Implied Terms

| Type | Test | Authority |
|------|------|------|
| **By Fact** | Business efficacy test + officious bystander test | *The Moorcock [1889]*, *Shirlaw v Southern Foundries [1939]* |
| | Singapore's 3-stage supplement: gap → necessity → obviousness | *Sembcorp Marine v PPL Holdings [2013]* |
| **By Law** | Naturally incident to the type of contract | *Liverpool City Council v Irwin [1977]* |
| **By Statute** | Automatically inserted by statute (e.g. SOGA) | [[wiki/statutes/sales-of-goods-act]] |
| **By Custom** | Industry practice, but cannot contradict an express term | *Hutton v Warren [1836]* |

## Classification of Terms
- **Condition (essential term)**: breach allows termination + damages
- **Warranty (subsidiary term)**: breach allows damages only
- **Innominate term (intermediate term)**: judged by the consequences (seriousness) of the breach → *Hong Kong Fir Shipping v Kawasaki [1962]*
- See [[wiki/law-concepts/discharge]]
