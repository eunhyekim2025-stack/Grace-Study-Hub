# Intention to Create Legal Relations (ICLR)

Even where there is an agreement, this determines whether both parties had an **intention to be legally bound**.

## Social & Domestic Agreements

**Presumption: no intention to be bound** (based on trust, love and affection) → *Balfour v Balfour [1947]*
- Rationale: saves the cost and time of litigation, and protects intimate relationships

### Rebuttal of the presumption
| Ground | Authority |
|------|------|
| Breakdown of the relationship (e.g. separation) | *Merritt v Merritt [1970]* |
| Involvement of a large sum (suggesting commercial intent) | *Simpkins v Pays [1955]*, *Peck v Lateu [1973]* |

## Business & Commercial Agreements

**Presumption: intention to be bound** → *Chua Kim Long v Phillip Securities [2006]*

### Rebuttal of the presumption
- **Honour clause**: expressly states there is no intention to create legal relations → *Rose & Frank Co v J R Crompton & Bros [1925]*
